Where to go

Six stops, north to south.

A working itinerary balances big cities with at least one quiet stop. Tokyo and Kyoto are the anchors; Hakone, Nara, Osaka, and Koyasan are the texture in between.

東京
Tokyo
First and last stop. Endless neighborhoods, the best food, the most ground to cover.
5 nights · base
箱根
Hakone
Onsen ryokan in the mountains. One overnight is plenty for the full hot-spring routine.
1 night · ryokan
京都
Kyoto
Temples, gardens, Pontocho dinners. The slow counterweight to Tokyo.
4 nights · culture
奈良
Nara
Day trip from Kyoto. Deer, the great Buddha, lunch, train back.
day trip
大阪
Osaka
Eat. That is the assignment. Dotonbori at night, Kuromon at lunch.
1 night · food
高野山
Koyasan
Sleep in a Buddhist temple on a mountain. Quiet, foggy, unforgettable.
1 night · temple stay

How to use this

It's a starting point.

Each city page has the things actually walked into — somewhere to stay, what to do, and where to eat. The itinerary shows the exact day-by-day if it helps you pace.

The map plots everything in one place; the logistics page covers rail passes, SIMs, and roughly what the trip cost.

JR Pass — cheaper if purchased outside Japan and activated on arrival.
teamLab — tickets are released by date and sell out.
