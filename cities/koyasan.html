Where we stayed

  • Stay

    Koyasan Syukubo Ekoin

    A working Shingon Buddhist temple that hosts foreign guests in tatami rooms. Highlights: a multi-course vegetarian dinner served in your room, a morning fire ceremony (goma) you can sit in on, optional morning meditation, and the excellent night cemetery tour led by a resident monk — this is the single most memorable thing on the trip. Book several months in advance, English bookings are easy through their website.

What to do

  • See

    Okunoin Cemetery

    Japan's largest cemetery — more than 200,000 moss-covered tombs spread across a 2km path through towering ancient cedars, leading to the mausoleum of Kobo Daishi. Walk it twice: once at night with the Ekoin monk-led tour for explanation and lantern light, and again at sunrise for the misty quiet.

  • See

    Garan & Kongobu-ji

    The two big temple complexes at the center of town — the vermillion Konpon Daito pagoda and the rock garden at Kongobu-ji. Walkable from any of the shukubo.

Getting there from Osaka

From Namba Station in Osaka: Nankai Koya line express to Gokurakubashi (~90 min) → cable car up the mountain (~5 min) → local bus into town (~10 min). The Koyasan-Sekai-Isan Kippu round-trip pass covers all three legs plus unlimited local buses on the mountain. JR Pass does not cover this leg.
