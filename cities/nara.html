The walk

  • See

    Nara Park

    Wide grassy park behind Kintetsu-Nara Station full of sika deer that will bow for a shika senbei cracker (¥200 a bundle from stands at the park edge). They're wild — respectful but pushy — so keep snacks zipped away.

  • See

    Todai-ji Daibutsu Hall

    Inside the park, the world's largest wooden building — housing the world's largest bronze Buddha. Worth the ticket. Try to crawl through the famous "Buddha's nostril" pillar on the way out.

  • See

    Kasuga Taisha

    Further into the park, the famous lantern shrine — thousands of bronze and stone lanterns lining the wooded approach. Easy add-on if you have time.

Getting there from Kyoto

Take the JR Nara line from Kyoto Station to Nara (~45 min, JR Pass covers it). The park is a 15-minute walk from the station. Plan 4–5 hours on the ground.
