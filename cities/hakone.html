Hakone

1 night between Tokyo and Kyoto · onsen ryokan stop

Hakone is a mountain hot-spring town two hours from Tokyo. You come here to soak in an onsen, eat kaiseki dinner in a yukata, sleep on a futon, soak again in the morning. One night is enough; the trip pretty much earns the ryokan budget on its own.

Onsen ryokan Mt. Fuji views (clear days) Hakone Free Pass

Where we stayed

  • Stay

    Laforet Club Hakone Gora

    Western-style hotel up in Gora with shared hot-spring baths. Less traditional than a full ryokan but a comfortable, well-priced version of the experience — the food, the yukatas, the baths all included. Walking distance to the open-air museum.

What to do

The classic Hakone routine is the round-the-loop tour using the Hakone Free Pass: mountain railway up, ropeway across the valley with views of Mt. Fuji and the sulfur vents at Owakudani, pirate boat across Lake Ashi, bus back. Each leg is short; the whole loop takes a relaxed half-day.

Worth working in: the Hakone Open-Air Museum in Gora (sculpture park with a Picasso pavilion), the Hakone Shrine torii rising out of Lake Ashi for a photo, and a stop at Owakudani for black eggs cooked in volcanic water.

Getting there from Tokyo

Tokyo Station → Odawara on the Kodama shinkansen (~35 min, covered by JR Pass) → switch to the Hakone Tozan line up the mountain. The Odakyu Hakone Free Pass from Shinjuku is the alternative if you don't have a JR Pass on this leg.
