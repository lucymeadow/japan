Osaka · 大阪

Osaka

1 night · the food city · gateway to Koyasan
大阪

Osaka is — with apologies — the eating stop. You can do real sightseeing here (Osaka Castle, the Umeda Sky Building) but most people come for the food, and rightly so. One overnight in Namba pairs cleanly with Koyasan, which leaves from the same station.

Namba base Eat-your-way Day trip from Kyoto

Where we stayed

  • Stay

    Fraser Residence Nankai Osaka

    Apartment-style hotel in Namba, walking distance to Dotonbori and Kuromon market, and one floor above the Nankai line platform to Koyasan. Perfect single-night stop.

What to do

  • See

    Dotonbori

    Osaka's neon canal-side eating street. Takoyaki, okonomiyaki, the Glico running man neon sign, the giant pufferfish. Best at night. Skip the chain restaurants with English barkers; line up at the ones full of locals.

  • See

    Kuromon Ichiba Market

    Osaka's "kitchen" — 600m of covered stalls selling A5 wagyu skewers, fresh uni, oysters on the shell. Go for lunch, very hungry. Eat as you walk.

Where we ate

  • Eat · $

    Ichiran Dotonbori Honkan

    The 24-hour tonkotsu ramen chain's flagship branch in Dotonbori. Order from a vending machine, eat in a solo booth, customize the broth and noodle firmness. Touristy but genuinely good and worth doing once.

The transit move

Osaka pairs naturally with Koyasan: stash your big bag in a Namba Station coin locker (or have your hotel hold it), take the Nankai express up the mountain, spend a night, come back down. The Nankai "Koyasan-Sekai-Isan Kippu" round-trip pass covers the train, cable car, and local buses on the mountain.
