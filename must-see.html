Must See

必見
  • Tokyo

    Shibuya Crossing

    The world's busiest pedestrian scramble. The view is much better from above — the Starbucks at Tsutaya is free and packed, the Shibuya Sky observation deck is paid and worth it.

  • Tokyo

    Tokyo City View & Sky Deck

    Roppongi Hills observatory. The Sky Deck on top is the only one in Tokyo where you're actually outside — open-air, no glass. Sunset is the move.

  • Tokyo

    Digital Art Museum (teamLab)

    Immersive room-scale digital art. Originally teamLab Borderless at Odaiba; the main venue is now at Azabudai Hills as of 2024. Tickets are timed and sell out — book before you fly. Plan two to three hours inside.

  • Tokyo

    Samurai Restaurant

    Kabukicho's legendary neon-and-robots dinner show. (The original Robot Restaurant closed during COVID; the Samurai Restaurant successor reopened in roughly the same venue and vein.) Loud, absurd, perfect with a group.

  • Kyoto

    Pontocho Alley

    Narrow lantern-lit cobbled lane along the Kamogawa, lined with restaurants from cheap yakitori to high-end kaiseki. Walk it at dusk; if you're there May–September, book somewhere with a yuka riverside terrace.

  • Kyoto

    Gion

    The old geisha district, just across the river from Pontocho. Wooden machiya houses, lantern light, tea houses. Be a respectful tourist — no chasing geiko down alleys for photos.

  • Kyoto

    Kyoto Day Tour

    A 6-hour private guided day (we used ToursByLocals) covered Kinkaku-ji, Ryoan-ji, and Kiyomizu-dera in one efficient sweep. Best on day one — everything else self-guided after.

  • Kyoto

    Fushimi Inari Taisha

    The thousand vermillion torii gates winding up the mountain. Free, open 24/7. Go before 8am or after sunset to skip the crowds — the lower gates are unwalkable by mid-morning. Hike at least to the Yotsutsuji intersection for the view.

  • Hakone

    Hakone Onsen

    One overnight in a mountain onsen ryokan, two hours from Tokyo by shinkansen. Soak, kaiseki dinner in a yukata, sleep on futon, soak again in the morning. The stay basically earns the ryokan budget on its own. (See the Hakone page for the full loop.)

  • Osaka

    Dotonbori

    Osaka's neon canal-side eating street — takoyaki, okonomiyaki, the Glico running man. Best at night. Skip the chain restaurants with English barkers; line up at the ones full of locals.
